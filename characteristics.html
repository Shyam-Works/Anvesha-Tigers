Physical Characteristics of Tigers

Tigers are part of the mammal family. More specifically, they are part of the Felidae family. Their scientific name is Panthera tigris. Tigers are large powerful creatures. They have orange bodies with black stripes. Did you know that each tiger's stripes are as unique as a human fingeprints? Tigers are powerfully built creatures, and the largest of the big cats. Tiger Behaviour

Tiger are really smart and stealthy. They are independent, solitary, and territorial creatures. Their hunting skills are top-notch. However, tigers are also very protective. Female tigers are constantly making sure that their cubs are safe. As for the cubs themselves, they are extremely cute and playful. Tiger cubs often play-fight to practise hunting. Tigers are really resilient, as they are able to easily adapt.

Tigers love to communicate with each other. They use a variety of vocal noises. Some examples are roars, growls, hisses, and chuffs. Chuffs are kind of like a friendly tiger noise, since they can't purr.

Tigers represent power, elegance, self-reliance, and determination. They are also revered in many Asian cultures. They are often depicted in mythology, art, and folklore.
