Habitat of a Tiger

Tigers live in a variety of natural habitat. From dense forests to open grasslands, these environments provide the space, food, and shelter they need to survive. This map here shows where the majestic tigers live. It is important to know where tigers live as they are under threat of going extinct.

In these regions where the tigers live, there is a large amount of diversity, from open grassslands, to dense jungles. Tigers live 95% of tigers live in Middle - Eastern Asia.

India

Tropical and Sub-tropical Forests

They are found in regions of Northern India. These dense forests provide thick foliage and abudant prey for the tigers to hunt and hide.

Mangrove Swamps

These swamps, are home to the infamous Bengal tigers. The coastal forests and tidal waters require tigers to swim more in these regions.

Grasslands and Savannahs

In parts of Northern and Central India, these open landforms provide prey in abudant populations, and lots of land for the majestic tigers to roam freely.

Arid Deciduous Forests

These forests have seasonal leaf shedding, making it easier for tigers to stalk prey.

Mountainous Forests

Located at the base of the Himalayan mountains, tigers have to adapt to the cooler temperatures and rugged terrain. Tigers are more rare in higher altitude regions.

Russia

Temparate Forests

These forests provide dense foliage for tigers to hunt and to take shelter in. There is a rich environment of prey.

Mountainous Terrain

The Sikhote-Alin mountain range offers steep valleys and vast forests where Amur tigers roam. The rugged landscape makes hunting more challenging but provides natural protection from human activity.

South-East Asia

Tropical Rainforests

Found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Myanmar, these dense forests provide ideal cover for tigers to stalk their prey while supporting rich biodiversity.

Mangrove Swamps

Coastal regions, particularly in parts of Myanmar and Indonesia, have mangrove forests where tigers navigate through waterlogged terrain in search of food.

Mountains

High-altitude forests, such as those in northern Thailand and Vietnam, offer rugged terrain where tigers can find shelter and remain hidden from human activity.
